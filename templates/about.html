About The Project

This project is a Web Application named ExoSimulator as its main focus is on Exoplanets and hence the name.

Exoplanets are worlds out of our solar system and currently out of our reach. We live in our solar system but we don't know how many such planetary systems that exist out there.

We have so much data and information about our own solar system, but does that help us generalize and tell us everything there is to know about planetary systems? Well... Not quite. You see our solar system is unique and has such amazing and interesting features but not all solar systems are the same. Analyzing data from space missions such as Kepler or TESS who have been able to find thousands of exoplanets orbiting other stars, helps us compare their properties, a good look at a wider range of data can help us understand to explore how planetary systems to function or how certain properties affect others (To Learn more - Go to our Data Science Page).

Sometimes it can also be extremely hard to visualize these planetary systems and understand how they actually look and function, what are their physical properties, although a simulation could potentially help to better visualize these systems and solve the issue making it easier for us to spot different properties and trends, understanding how the planetary system actually looks like and what it would be like to stay in such a system or planet (To Learn more - Go to our Simulation).

Finally Imagine if you could design your own system based on real-life data and models, how changing a certain value affects the system and what changes it makes, perfecting and determining the exact values to possibly even make our own solar system. This is where a combination of data science and simulation would be required and hence the playground mode was created to view the influence of one property on another (To Learn more - Go to our Playground Mode).
