Data Analysis Overview

This Web Page has been split into 2 parts -

  1. Histogram and trend or pattern analyses - In this type of analysis, we plot histograms with different bin sizes to explore the trends and patterns in the properties of exoplanets and how they occur naturally and how the way we detect exoplanets affect our data.
  2. Predictive or regression analyses - Here we create scatter plots of 2 properties, to observe and see how and if they relate to each other. What trend can we observe between the properties and through one property is it possible to predict the other. We also explore how a certain property may affect another and why does this happen.

Upon a glance at the graph, we can see that the most used method or most planets have been detected by the Transit method as it is easy to use, requires minimal equipment and has the best chance of detecting a planet, while the radial velocity method comes in second again with the same reasons of easy detection, minimal equipment and higher detection rate. While the other methods are hard to conduct, require special equipment or have low rates of detecting a planet although are yet used to gather special data on the planet or system that the other methods would not have been able to provide. This graph and data are vital to the results that the other graphs about planetary and stellar data provide as the method used are also extremely important. Depending on the method, different types of systems can be detected, and therefore a variety of methods exist out there to detect exoplanets.

Stellar Spectral Types

This graph talks about the spectral types of stars and is extremely important to the next couple of graphs, properties and relations that are to be drawn. The spectral type of a star provides us with enough information to derive a lot of information. A spectral type can help understand the range of radii, mass and temperature the star lies in. It can even tell us about its colour, its phase in the stellar life cycle and much more. Each spectral type defines a set of properties and there are various spectral types. Each star must fall in at least one of the spectral types.
Through this graph of spectral types, we can see a huge amount of stars in the lower end meaning the spectral types of M, K, G, and F while the other spectral types are quite low. The graph starts low at M, Peaks at G and constantly declines towards the higher spectral types. due to this, we are also bound to look at normal distributions in multiple properties.
Due to the properties that these spectral types yield, the graph is expected as M, K, G and F spectral types have smaller radii, mass and have a lower temperature as well, making it easier for them to afford planets and make a planetary system, our star, The Sun is of spectral type G and therefore can efficiently have an 8 planet system with one of them being habitable as well.

Semi-Major Axis and Eccentricity to Planet Periapsis Velocity

The semi-major axis and the eccentricity are extremely vital parts of information when defining the orbit of a planet, they as well play a major role in determining the velocity of the planet at any point of the orbit. As eccentricity increases the velocity at the periapsis also increases as the orbit is so flattened therefore it spends more time closer to the sun. and because gravity's equation also takes into account the distance between 2 bodies with higher eccentricity more time is spent closer to the sun compared to lower eccentricity where the distance is quite divided or equal and therefore as the eccentricity increases so does the velocity at the periapsis.
While a similar justification goes for the semi-major axis as larger orbits require lower velocity to stay in orbit and not fall into the star while smaller orbits require more velocity to revolve around their sun due to the distance and the force of gravity being stronger. When combining the 2 in a machine learning algorithm we can see and understand as we provide the 2 inputs we receive a meaningful output. This model created was also tested and verified with planets of the solar system and they did provide quite close answers.
